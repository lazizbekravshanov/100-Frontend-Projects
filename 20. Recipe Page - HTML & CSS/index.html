Lemon Garlic Butter Pasta

A bright, 20-minute pasta built on a silky emulsion of butter, garlic and lemon. It leans on pantry staples yet tastes like something you'd order out — finished with parmesan and a fistful of parsley.

  • Prep 10 min
  • Cook 15 min
  • Serves 4
  • Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

For 4 servings

  • 12 oz (340 g) spaghetti or linguine
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • ¾ cup pasta cooking water, reserved
  • ¾ cup grated parmesan, plus more to serve
  • ½ cup flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  • Sea salt & black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the pasta

    Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta until just shy of al dente, then reserve ¾ cup of the starchy water before draining.

  2. Bloom the garlic

    Meanwhile, melt the butter with the olive oil in a wide pan over medium-low. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes; cook gently until fragrant and pale gold, about 2 minutes. Don't let the garlic brown.

  3. Build the sauce

    Add the lemon zest and juice, then pour in half the reserved pasta water. Swirl the pan to emulsify into a glossy sauce.

  4. Toss and emulsify

    Add the drained pasta and parmesan. Toss vigorously over low heat, splashing in more pasta water as needed, until the sauce clings to every strand.

  5. Finish and serve

    Off the heat, fold in the parsley and season with salt and plenty of black pepper. Plate immediately with extra parmesan and a final grind of pepper.

Tip: Keep the heat low once the parmesan goes in — too hot and it clumps instead of melting into a smooth sauce.
